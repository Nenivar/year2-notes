Strictly Dominated Strategies

Pick blue - no matter what your pair does, you'll get more points that way.

My strategy iii strictly dominates my strategy jjj, if my payoff from iii is always strictly greater than from jjj, regardless of what my opponent does

→\rightarrow→ do not play a strictly dominated strategy

Nash Equilibrium

A pair of mixed strategies (p,q)(p,q)(p,q) for the row and column players respectively is called a Nash Equilibrium.

Nash Equilibrium

If neither player can increase her payoff by unilaterally deviating from her strategy.

Formal definition:

A nash equilibrium for a 2-player game is a pair (p,q)(p,q)(p,q) with ppp in SR,qS_R,qSR​,q in SCS_CSC​ satisfying:

  • R[p,q]≥R[p′,q]R[p,q] \geq R[p',q]R[p,q]≥R[p′,q] for all p′∈SRp' \in S_Rp′∈SR​
  • C[p,q]≥C[p,q′]C[p,q] \geq C[p,q']C[p,q]≥C[p,q′] for all q′∈SCq' \in S_Cq′∈SC​

Players don't always play NE, but:

  • No regrets: if you played a NE, you could say afterwards 'given what the other player did, I have no regrets'
  • Self-fulfilling belief if I believe the other player will play their part of a NE, I can't get a better payoff than if I play my part - so I play my part

Every game that can be specified by a payoff matrix has a Nash Equilibrium (may have more than one)
